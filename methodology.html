How We Score Risk

An open, composable methodology for evaluating the risk of DeFi yield strategies. Every assumption is public, every score is auditable.

Step 1

Decompose the Strategy

Every strategy is broken down into up to three layers, each representing a different level of the stack you are exposed to.

◈

Asset

The underlying token itself. Is USDC safe? How stable is stETH? What backs wBTC?

◎

Market

The lending or borrowing venue where the asset is deployed — Aave, Compound, Fluid, etc.

◆

Vault

The strategy vault or CDP that allocates capital across markets — Morpho, Yearn, Maker, etc.

Step 2

Evaluate on Three Axes

Each layer is independently scored on three dimensions. Together they form a 3 × 3 matrix of scores.

🔒

Security

Smart contract maturity, audit coverage, bug bounty programs, and track record (Lindy effect).

⚙

Operations

Governance structure, timelocks, oracle quality, documentation transparency, and regulatory posture.

⚖

Economics

Peg stability, collateral quality, liquidity depth, strategy complexity, and liquidation risk.

Security Operations Economics
Asset 0 – 10 0 – 10 0 – 10
Market 0 – 10 0 – 10 0 – 10
Vault 0 – 10 0 – 10 0 – 10

Each cell is scored 0–10 by independent risk providers. Higher means safer. Missing data defaults to 0 — opacity is a risk factor.

Step 3

Compute the Score

Layer scores are weighted averages of their three axes. The weights reflect what matters most at each level:

Asset & Market

Security 30% · Operations 30% · Economics 40%

Vault

Security 40% · Operations 40% · Economics 20%

Strategy

Weighted combination of applicable layers, depending on strategy type.

The final output is a single 0–10 score for the entire strategy, always accompanied by the full axis breakdown so you can see exactly where risk concentrates.

Context Layer

Counterparty Exposure Map

A numeric score tells you how much risk. The Counterparty Exposure Map tells you where it comes from. Every scored strategy is accompanied by a map of all protocols and entities it depends on.

For each counterparty we record:

Exposure

Major — value flows directly through this protocol. Minor — auxiliary dependency like an oracle or bridge.

Lindy Score

How battle-tested is this protocol? Based on TVL secured multiplied by time without a critical exploit.

Operations Level

Governance maturity sourced from DeFiScan or equivalent frameworks. How decentralized and transparent is the protocol?

Example: a Morpho USDC vault deployed on Aave

Counterparty Layer Exposure Lindy Operations
Circle (USDC) Asset Major High Medium
Aave v3 Market Major High High
Morpho Vault Major Medium Medium
Chainlink Market Minor High High

Lindy Tiers

High > $1B TVL, 3+ years, no exploit
Medium > $100M TVL, 1+ year, no exploit
Low < $100M or < 1 year or exploited

Operations Tiers

High DeFiScan Stage 2+, fully decentralized
Medium DeFiScan Stage 1, partial decentralization
Low DeFiScan Stage 0, centralized control
The map is not a scoring input. It is a transparency layer. A 7/10 strategy backed by all-High-Lindy counterparties tells a very different story than a 7/10 with Low-Lindy dependencies. The map gives you the context the number alone cannot.

Trust Model

Multiple Independent Raters

No single company defines risk. Multiple independent risk providers submit scores for each cell. The Alliance aggregates them into a single score using a transparent weighted average.

A rater can cover as little as one axis for one layer — there is no requirement to cover everything. Each submission participates independently. Rater weights are documented and versioned so that every score can be traced back to its sources.

If no rater covers a cell, the default-to-worse rule applies: the score is 0. Opacity is always penalized.
